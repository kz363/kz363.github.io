Dealing With Conflict

April 5, 2014 by Kevin

  1. Discuss a time you had an emotional conflict with someone. What was it about? What happened?

    2. I created a Starcraft team in college and we used to communicate with each other with an e-mail discussion thread. There was a player, R, on the team who used to be active, but stopped playing and switched to playing League of Legends. One of the other players asked a question about the game and R responded with an answer that I knew was wrong, so I sent a link in the e-mail thread proving him wrong. He went livid and we had an argument through public e-mails.

  2. What was the source of the conflict?

    3. I said his answer was misinformed because he doesn't play the game anymore. I've joked with him in the past about him switching his loyalty to a different game but it seemed like it ticked him off a lot more this time. His first response was a lot of cussing at me, so I knew he was in a bad mood already. He started pointing out other e-mails I sent in the past that sounded derogatory, which the rest of the team told him that he misinterpreted because my e-mails consist of snarky humor.

  3. How did you handle that conflict?

    4. He kept attacking me personally, to which I felt obliged to defend myself. This went out of hand and I tried to keep our argument on track. Because he never addressed the actual problem, which was his answer being wrong, I kept asking him if he actually clicked the link I sent but he ignored me.

  4. Did your actions make the conflict better or worse?

    5. Definitely worse. I knew I was right, the rest of the team knew I was right, but he wasn't in the right state of mind and didn't care that I was right. Nothing I said could've deescalated the situation, but I couldn't keep my mouth shut.

  5. If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

    6. I wouldn't have engaged in the argument with someone who was clearly having a bad day. He normally isn't that irritable.

  6. What did you learn from this experience?

    7. When people are hotheaded beyond reason, it's best to not push their buttons and avoid conflict.
